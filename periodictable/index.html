Definitions:

- Elements:
A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means. An element is composed of atoms that have the same atomic number, that is, each atom has the same number of protons in its nucleus as all other atoms of that element.


- Atomic Number:
The number of positive charges or protons in the nucleus of an atom of a given element, and therefore also the number of electrons normally surrounding the nucleus.


- Average Atomic Mass:
The average atomic mass of an element is the sum of the masses of its isotopes, each multiplied by its natural abundance (the decimal associated with percent of atoms of that element that are of a given isotope).


- Protons:
A positively charged elementary particle that is a fundamental constituent of all atomic nuclei.


- Neutrons:
An elementary particle having no charge, mass slightly greater than that of a proton.


- Electrons:
An elementary particle that is a fundamental constituent of matter, having a negative charge, and existing independently or as the component outside the nucleus of an atom.


- Isotopes:
Any of two or more forms of a chemical element, having the same number of protons in the nucleus, or the same atomic number, but having different numbers of neutrons in the nucleus, or different atomic weights.


- Ions:
An electrically charged atom or group of atoms formed by the loss or gain of one or more electrons, as a cation (positive ion) which is created by electron loss and is attracted to the cathode in electrolysis, or as an anion (negative ion) which is created by an electron gain and is attracted to the anode.



Periodic Trends:

Atomic Radius:

- Atomic size increases down a column because there are more protons and more energy levels for electrons to occupy.

- Atomic size decreases across a period because electrons are adde into the same because electrons are added into the same energy level; the increasing + charge of the nucleus pulls the electrons closer to the nucleu - making the atom smaller.

Exceptions:

- Noble gases: noble gases actually relax a bit once they have their octet.


Ionization energy:

- Ionization energy - the energy required to remove an electron from an atom.

- Ionization energy increases from left to right on the periodic table due to the number of valence electrons and the octet rule.

- Ionization energy decreases from top to bottom within a group (column) due to the increasing distance the electrons are from the nucleus.

- Energy required to remove the second and third electrons is the second and third ionization energy, respectively.

Exceptions:

- Half filled orbitals - in some of the larger subshells stability is gained at the halfway point of filling.


Electronegativity:

- Electronegativity - the ability of an atom to attract an electron or electrons when in a compound.

- The electronegativity values follow the same trends as the ionization energy. Increases from left to right, decreases from top to bottom within a group

- The electron affinity values follow the same trends as the ionization energy and electronegativity. Increases from left to right, decreases from top to bottom within a group.
